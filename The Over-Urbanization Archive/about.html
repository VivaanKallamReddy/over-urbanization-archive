About The Founder

The story behind a student-built global archive documenting the impacts of over-urbanization.

A Student With A Vision

This archive website was founded by a very young individual from the Aga Khan Academy, Hyderabad in the ninth grade. One day Vivaan was sitting in his Individual and Societies class and was given the task to create a website related to one of the topics he learned that year.

Instead of creating a simple project just for school, Vivaan decided to follow his passion and build something meaningful that could genuinely educate people and help others interested in urbanization studies.

While researching online, he observed that there were not many large-scale urbanization archives available where scholars, students, or researchers could easily access organized case studies. This inspired him to create a platform focused specifically on over-urbanization.

He conducted rigorous background research on cities around the world and analyzed the social, economic, political, and environmental impacts of rapid urban growth.

What began as a classroom task slowly transformed into a detailed digital archive dedicated to understanding one of the most important global challenges of the modern era.

“Cities define the future of humanity. Understanding over-urbanization is essential if we want to build sustainable societies for future generations.”
