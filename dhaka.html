Dhaka, Bangladesh

A rapidly growing megacity facing climate migration, overcrowding, pollution, and infrastructure pressure.

Urban Growth In Dhaka

Dhaka is one of the fastest-growing cities in the world. Millions of migrants moved into the city because of poverty, flooding, and climate-related displacement from rural areas.

The city’s garment industry created major employment opportunities, but infrastructure systems failed to expand quickly enough to support the growing population.

Overcrowding, traffic congestion, pollution, and inadequate housing became major challenges across the city. Dhaka demonstrates the connection between climate migration and over-urbanization.

Social Impacts

Millions of residents live in overcrowded informal settlements with poor sanitation and limited access to healthcare and clean water.

Air pollution and contaminated water create serious health risks for low-income communities. Respiratory diseases and waterborne illnesses remain common.

Traffic congestion also reduces quality of life because workers spend several hours travelling through crowded roads each day.

Economic Impacts

Dhaka’s garment industry became a major driver of Bangladesh’s economy and created employment for millions of workers.

However, congestion and infrastructure strain reduce productivity and increase transportation costs across the city.

Informal housing and overcrowding demonstrate how economic growth has not fully improved living conditions for many residents.

Political Impacts

Dhaka’s rapid growth places enormous pressure on government authorities to improve housing, transport, and disaster management systems.

Climate migration increased political concerns about long-term urban planning and sustainable development.

Weak enforcement of building regulations and poor environmental protections continue to create governance challenges across the city.

Environmental Impacts

Air pollution from vehicles, factories, and waste burning severely affects public health and air quality.

Rivers surrounding the city became heavily polluted due to industrial waste and poor waste management systems.

Wetlands and green spaces disappeared as urban expansion accelerated, increasing flood risks during monsoon seasons.

“Dhaka demonstrates how climate migration and rapid urban expansion can overwhelm infrastructure systems and environmental stability.”
